MISSILE MAN OF INDIA

The Missile Man of India: Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the "People's President," was a man of extraordinary vision, integrity, and dedication. Born on October 15, 1931, in the town of Rameswaram on Pamban Island, Tamil Nadu, India, Kalam came from a humble background . Despite his modest beginnings, he rose to become one of India's most celebrated Presidents and a renowned scientist, making significant contributions to the country's economic and social development . This article will delve into the life, achievements, and legacy of this remarkable individual.

Early Life and Education:

Kalam was the youngest of five siblings, and his father was a boat owner while his mother was a housewife
He started school at an early age and completed his schooling in Ramanathapuram Hindu Middle School and Schwartz Higher Secondary School before moving on to St. Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli, where he studied physics and mathematics . He then went on to the Indian Institute of Technology in Madras

Career and Achievements:

After completing his education, Kalam started his career as a scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), where he worked on various projects . In 1963, he transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he played a pivotal role in the development of India's first satellite . He continued to work with ISRO for many years, contributing to the successful launch of several satellites, including the Aryabhata-1 in 1975, India's first satellite . In 1980, Kalam returned to DRDO, where he took up the challenge of developing indigenous missile technology for India's defense program . Under his leadership, DRDO successfully developed several missiles, including the Agni and Prithvi series . He also played a key role in increasing cooperation between India and Russia in the field of space research . In 1992, Kalam was appointed as the Scientific Adviser to the Defense Minister, a position he held until 1999 . During this period, he contributed significantly to the modernization of India's armed forces and the expansion of its nuclear arsenal . He also helped establish India as a nuclear power and oversaw the development of several indigenous missiles . Kalam's crowning achievement came in the form of the SLV-III, India's maiden indigenous satellite launch vehicle, marking a significant milestone in the country's space exploration endeavors . The world recognized his invaluable contributions by bestowing upon him the prestigious title of "Missile Man of India"

Presidency and Legacy:

Kalam was elected as the 11th president of India in 2002 with the support of both the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party and the then-opposition Indian National Congress . Widely referred to as the "People's President," he returned to his civilian life of education, writing, and public service after a single term . He was a recipient of several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor . During his tenure as President, Kalam made significant contributions to the country in the areas of science and technology, defense, and education . He was a strong advocate for women's rights and worked to empower women across India . His humility, wisdom, and accessibility made him a beloved leader during his tenure as President from 2002 to 2007 Kalam was known for his inspiring speeches and writings, which continue to captivate the hearts of young readers and inspire countless individuals to dream and chase their dreams . His autobiography, "Wings of Fire," and other books, such as "Indomitable Spirit" and "Envisioning an Empowered Nation," provide valuable insights into his life, values, and ideals .

Dr. A P J Abdul Kalam's Biography - Kalam as a Scientist:

Kalam joined as a scientist in the aeronautical development establishment of DRDO in 1960, after graduation. His career started with him designing a small hovercraft. However, he was not convinced by his choice of a job at the DRDO. Kalam was transferred to ISRO in 1969, where he was the project director of India's first satellite vehicle launch. The satellite vehicle deployed the Rohini satellite successfully in near-Earth orbit in July 1980. Kalam received the Government's LV and Slv projects between the 1970s-90s. He directed two projects like Project Devil and Project Valiant, which aimed at developing ballistic missiles from the successful SLV program's technology Kalam somehow convinced Indira Gandhi and sought secret funds for these aerospace projects. His research and immense knowledge brought him and the nation great laurels in the 1980s. Kalam then went on to become the scientific advisor of the defence minister in 1992 and served at the same post for five years before getting promoted to the post of principal scientific advisor to the government. His immense role in the country's 1998 nuclear weapons tests solidified India as a nuclear power. Kamal had now become a national hero, to be remembered for ages to come. However, the tests he conducted caused a huge uproar in the international community. Kamal put forward a nationwide plan called Technology Vision 2020, which according to him, was a fantastic way for transforming the stature of India in 20 years, taking it from a developing to a developed nation. The plan envisioned the progress of the nation by adopting advanced technology, expanding healthcare facilities, and emphasizing the education of the masses.

Conclusion

A. P. J. Abdul Kalam was a remarkable individual who left an indelible mark on India and the world. His contributions to science, technology, defense, and education have shaped the country's progress in many ways, from pushing technological advancement to inspiring generations of dreamers with his words of wisdom . As we remember him fondly, we must continue to celebrate his achievements and honor his remarkable legacy.
